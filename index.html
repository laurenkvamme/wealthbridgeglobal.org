Our Mission

Financial literacy is a behavior problem, not a knowledge problem

We live in a world engineered for overconsumption. Frictionless payments, endless scroll, buy-now-pay-later, one-click checkout, and the list keeps growing.

Financial literacy programs have tried to solve this with information. We believe the real gap is not knowledge, it is experience. Students do not need to be told that instant gratification is powerful—they need to practice decisions under realistic conditions.

Through hands-on Decision Labs grounded in behavioral science, we help students practice the moments that matter, before the stakes are real.

About Us

We are rethinking how financial literacy is taught

Lauren Kvamme
Founder and Executive Director

Lauren Kvamme attends Harvard-Westlake School and is the founder of Wealth Bridge Global, a behavioral finance education initiative she started after partnering with Junior Achievement.

Over spring break 2026, Lauren conducted comparative research in Costa Rica, studying how different payment systems and financial environments shape the money habits young people form early in life.

Lauren has met with advisors at Goldman Sachs and Delta as well as connected with researchers at UCLA. Outside of Wealth Bridge, Lauren is passionate about economics and behavioral science.

What We Believe

Three principles behind everything we build

01
Experience over information
Students learn by doing, not by hearing. Every Wealth Bridge module puts students in a real decision scenario before it explains the concept behind it.
02
Behavior over knowledge
The gap between knowing what to do and actually doing it is where financial outcomes are decided. We design for behavior change, not comprehension.
03
Access over exclusivity
The students who most need behavioral financial tools are the ones least likely to receive them. We bring this work directly into under-resourced communities.

Our Story

From a classroom to a movement

Wealth Bridge Global began as a student-led financial education initiative through Junior Achievement SoCal. What started as a team of high schoolers teaching younger students quickly grew into a research-backed, student-powered program.

That observation became the foundation for everything Wealth Bridge has built since, from comparative research conducted in Costa Rica to the behavioral Decision Labs now in development.
